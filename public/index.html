Understanding Elections

An informed vote is a powerful vote. Learn how Indian elections are structured and how to find trustworthy information about candidates.

Types of Elections

The Lok Sabha (House of the People) is the lower house of India's Parliament. It has 543 elected seats. Members are directly elected by citizens through first-past-the-post voting in single-member constituencies. elections choose Members of Parliament. Vidhan Sabha elections choose MLAs for state governments. Panchayat and Municipal elections govern at the local level.

Election Commission

The Election Commission of India (ECI) is an autonomous constitutional body responsible for administering elections in India. It enforces the Model Code of Conduct, manages the electoral roll, and oversees the entire voting process. is the autonomous body that conducts free and fair elections across India. It enforces the Model Code of Conduct.

Phase 3

Polling Day: Cast Your Vote

It's your democratic right. Head to your designated polling booth, verify your identity, and cast your vote on the EVM.

🖥️

Electronic Voting Machines (EVMs) are used in all Indian elections. They are standalone, battery-operated devices that record votes electronically. Each EVM can handle up to 64 candidates.

Press the button next to your candidate's name and symbol. The VVPAT slip confirms your choice.

📮

Postal ballots are available for select categories: armed forces personnel, government employees on election duty, persons with disabilities, senior citizens (80+), and COVID-affected voters. Apply through Form 12.

Phase 4

Counting & Results

After all phases of voting are complete, EVMs are stored securely. Here's what happens on counting day and after.

  1. Counting Day: EVMs are opened in the presence of candidates' agents. Postal ballots are counted first.
  2. Round-by-Round: Results are announced EVM counting happens in rounds. Each round covers a set of EVMs from different polling stations. Trends are updated on the ECI results portal in real-time as each round is completed. on the ECI results portal, with trends updated in real-time.
  3. VVPAT Verification: A mandatory sample of VVPAT slips is cross-verified against EVM counts to ensure accuracy.
  4. Declaration: The Returning Officer declares the winning candidate via Form 21C after final tallying.
  5. Government Formation: The party or alliance with majority (272+ in Lok Sabha) is invited to form the government.
