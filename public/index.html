Types of Elections

An election within a political party to choose who will represent that party in the general election. Open primaries let any voter participate; closed primaries are party-members only. narrow the field. General elections decide winners. Local elections happen year-round.

Ballot Measures

Laws or policy changes that citizens vote on directly, bypassing the legislature. Also called propositions, initiatives, or referendums depending on the state. let you vote directly on laws, not just candidates. Read the full text.

Phase 4

The Count & Certification

The polls close, but democracy doesn't stop. Here's what happens between Election Night and Inauguration Day.

  1. Election Night: Media projections are called, but they are not official.
  2. Weeks 1–3: All ballots (including mail-in) are officially counted and certified by each state.
  3. December: The A body of 538 electors who formally elect the President. Each state gets electors equal to its Congressional delegation. A candidate needs 270 electoral votes to win. electors cast official votes in their state capitals.
  4. January 6: Congress counts and certifies the Electoral College results.
  5. January 20: Inauguration Day — the new President is sworn in.
